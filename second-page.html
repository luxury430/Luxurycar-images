Most demanded in Auctions
Ferrari 250 GTO
  • Engine & Performance: Powered by a 3.0-liter Tipo 168/62 Colombo V12 engine, producing roughly 300 horsepower at 7,400 rpm. It featured a 5-speed manual transmission.
  • Design & Aerodynamics: Created by Giotto Bizzarrini, Mauro Forghieri, and Sergio Scaglietti, the car was designed with a focus on aerodynamics to control airflow, featuring a distinctive rear spoiler and improved cooling, which significantly increased top speed.
  • Racing Dominance: The 250 GTO won the International Championship for GT Manufacturers in 1962, 1963, and 1964. It dominated prestigious races like the Tour de France, Targa Florio, and Le Mans.
  • Rarity & Value: With only 36 produced, these cars are highly sought after by collectors, often fetching tens of millions of dollars at auction, with some selling for as high as $70 million.
  • Road Usage: Although designed as a race car, the 250 GTO was technically a road-legal Grand Tourer.

Most different looking hypercar
Apollo Evo
  • Engine: 6.3-liter naturally aspirated V12 producing approximately 800 hp (596 kW) and 765 Nm of torque.
  • Performance: 0-60 mph (97 km/h) in 2.7 seconds, with a top speed of 208 mph (335 km/h).

  • Production: Limited to only 10 units worldwide, with a focus on track performance. Transmission: Racing-style sequential transmission.
Still Mysterious Car
Lost bugatti SC atlantic type 57
  • Identity: Known as "La Voiture Noire" (The Black Car), it was the second of four Type 57SC Atlantics produced between 1936 and 1938.
  • Disappearance: It vanished while being transported by train from Molsheim to a safer location in Bordeaux to escape the German invasion in 1940.
  • Theories: Speculation ranges from it being destroyed during the war, hidden in a private collection, or buried.
  • Significance: It was a masterpiece of Art Deco design featuring a distinctive riveted ridge, with three of the four cars accounted for today.
  • It was the personal car of Jean Bugatti, one of only four ever produced, and is considered the "holy grail" of missing automobiles, with an estimated value exceeding million.

  • Performance: The vehicle is built on a custom coachbuild chassis powered by a V12 engine. Exclusivity: As one of the most expensive new cars in the world, priced estimated over $30 million (£20M+), it is the first of only four unique Droptail models commissioned.
