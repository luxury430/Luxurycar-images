Top Luxury Brands

(1955) MERCEDES
$142m - $148m
  • Extreme Rarity: Only two prototypes were built, both originally retained by Mercedes-Benz.
  • Unprecedented Provenance: It was designed by and served as the personal company car of Rudolf Uhlenhaut, the chief engineer.
  • Racing Pedigree & Performance: Based on the W196 R Grand Prix car that won two world championships, it was considered the fastest road-legal car of its time.
  • Historical Significance: It represents the peak of post-war German engineering and Mercedes' dominance in motorsport.
  • "Mona Lisa" of Cars: The combination of its beauty, gullwing doors, and unique history has cemented it as the most desirable"holy grail" car for collectors.
(1962) FERRARI 250 GTO
$39m - $70m
  • Highest Sale Price: $70 million+ (Private sale, 2018).
  • Highest Auction Price: $51.7 million (Late 2024).
  • Key Features: These vehicles were built between 1962 and 1964 for racing, featuring 3.0-liter V12 engines and rare, hand-crafted bodies.
  • Provenance: Top-tier examples often include race wins like the Tour de France and high finishes at Le Mans
Lamborghini Egoista
$100m - $117m
  • Design Inspiration: The exterior is modeled after Apache helicopters and fighter jets, featuring active aerodynamic panels that adjust for stability.
  • Cockpit & Interior: A single, central seat is surrounded by a "survival cell" made of carbon fiber and aluminum. The steering wheel must be removed to enter/exit, similar to Formula 1 cars.
  • Performance: The car is powered by a 5.2-liter V10 engine, producing 600 horsepower.
  • Features: It includes orange-tinted glass, unique LED lighting, and a head-up display.
  • Status: It is a non-street-legal, one-of-a-kind, functional prototype, sometimes reported as being valued at over $100 million.
Bugatti Type 57SC atlantic
$30m - $100m
  • Estimated Value: Analysts and experts place the value above $100 million due to its rarity and legendary status.
  • The Car: It was the personal car of Jean Bugatti and is considered a "lost Nazi treasure".
  • Other Atlantics: While the lost car is worth a projected $100M+, surviving models have previously sold for over $30 million to $40 million.

  • Key Features: These vehicles were built between 1962 and 1964 for racing, featuring 3.0-liter V12 engines and rare, hand-crafted bodies.
  • Provenance: Top-tier examples often include race wins like the Tour de France and high finishes at Le Mans
Koenigsegg one : 1
$7m - $10m
  • Extreme Exclusivity: Only seven examples were produced (including one prototype), making it one of the rarest modern hypercars.
  • 1:1 Power-to-Weight Ratio: It was the first production car to achieve a 1-megawatt (1,360 hp) power output for a 1,360 kg curb weight (1 PS per 1 kg), earning it the "megacar" title.
  • Bespoke Engineering: Features custom-developed parts like 3D-printed titanium exhaust components, active aerodynamics, and full carbon fiber wheels.
  • Track Performance: Engineered to produce 610 kg of downforce at 160 mph, allowing it to corner at 2g.
  • Rapid Appreciation: Because it represents a significant milestone in automotive history, the value of the One:1 has risen significantly over time, often doubling or tripling its original price in private sales.
  • "Megacar" Status: It was designed to outperform everything on the road, with a top speed theoretically over 270 mph (440 km/h).
